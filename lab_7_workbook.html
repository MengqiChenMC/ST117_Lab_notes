2. Mean Squared Error

Consider a random sample \(X_1,X_2,\ldots,X_n\) from a normal distribution with mean \(\mu\) and variance \(\sigma^2\). We have the following two estimators of the population variance obtained from the given sample information. From our previous lab, we derived the Maximum Likelihood Estimator(MLE) of the population variance as: \[ 1.\ \hat{\sigma}^2=\frac{1}{n}\sum_{i=1}^{n}(X_i-\overline{X})^2. \] Additionally, we have the sample variance given by: \[ 2.\ s^2=\frac{1}{n-1}\sum_{i=1}^{n}(X_i-\overline{X})^2, \] where \(\overline{X}\) represents the sample mean.

  1. Simulate a sample of 10 data from \(\text{N}(160,10^2)\). Calculate the values of these two estimators and their Mean Squared Error(MSE).
